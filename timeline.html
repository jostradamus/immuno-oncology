Approval Timeline

Tracing the evolution of immuno-oncology from early pioneers to the impending approval of individualized mRNA vaccines.

2010

The Pioneer

FDA approval of Sipuleucel-T (Provenge) for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer. It was the first-ever therapeutic cancer vaccine, proving that training the immune system was a viable clinical strategy.

2015

The Checkpoint Revolution

Widespread approval of immune checkpoint inhibitors (PD-1/PD-L1). These drugs proved the immune system could be effectively weaponized against tumors, setting the essential stage for future combination vaccine therapies.

2023

The Breakthrough

Moderna & Merck report ground-breaking KEYNOTE-942 Phase 2b data, proving that personalized mRNA vaccines (mRNA-4157) significantly reduce recurrence in high-risk melanoma.

2026

The Manufacturing Shift

Fast Track & Breakthrough Therapy designations are granted by the FDA as AI heavily reduces "vein-to-vein" manufacturing time to under 4 weeks, making individualized therapies logistically viable for large clinical trials.

2027+

The First Approvals (Projected)

Anticipated FDA approval for the first individualized mRNA neoantigen cancer vaccine as an adjuvant therapy for high-risk solid tumors, marking a new era in precision oncology.
