Major Neoantigen Targets

Exploring the specific genetic mutations driving cancer and how they are targeted by next-generation mRNA vaccines.

Shared (Public) Neoantigens

"Off-the-shelf" vaccines target driver mutations that are frequently shared across multiple patients and various cancer types. Because these genes are critical for tumor survival, the cancer cannot easily downregulate them to escape the immune system.

Personalized (Private) Neoantigens

While shared targets allow for off-the-shelf treatments, the vast majority of cancer mutations are unique to the individual patient ("passenger mutations").

The AI Prediction Pipeline

For personalized vaccines, the patient's tumor genome and healthy genome are sequenced and compared. AI algorithms (like NetMHCpan) scan thousands of unique mutations to predict which 10-30 neoantigens have the highest affinity for the patient's specific HLA (MHC) molecules. A custom mRNA sequence is then synthesized to encode precisely those targets, creating an individualized "n-of-1" therapeutic.
